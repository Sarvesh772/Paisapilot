Salary Slip Explained: CTC, In-hand, Deductions and Tax

Last updated: March 5, 2026

Your salary slip is not just an HR document. It controls your monthly budget, tax liability, loan eligibility, and even future increment negotiations.

Quick Answer

Track three numbers every month: gross salary, total deductions, and net in-hand. Budget should run on net in-hand only.

Key Terms in Plain Language

TermMeaningWhy it matters
CTCTotal annual cost to companyNot equal to take-home pay
Gross PayEarnings before deductionsBase for statutory cuts
Net PayFinal amount creditedUse this for budgeting
PF/EPFRetirement deductionLong-term savings + tax impact
TDSTax deducted at sourceAffects refund or extra tax payable

Monthly Salary Slip Audit

  1. Check net pay vs previous month.
  2. Verify TDS consistency with projected annual income.
  3. Confirm PF and professional tax deductions.
  4. Check if variable pay/arrears are correctly shown.

How to Use Salary Slip Better

  • Set SIP and savings automation on salary day.
  • Use average last 6-month in-hand for lifestyle decisions.
  • Avoid EMI commitments based on bonus-heavy months.
  • Keep digital archive for loan and visa documents.

FAQ

Why is my in-hand much lower than CTC?
Because CTC includes benefits and employer-side cost components that are not directly paid monthly.

Should I budget with expected bonus?
No. Treat bonus as irregular income and allocate separately.
