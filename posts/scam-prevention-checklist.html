Scam Prevention Checklist: 10 Practical Rules to Protect Your Money

Last updated: March 30, 2026

Most scams do not work because criminals are highly technical. They work because they create urgency, confusion, or fear. A simple scam prevention checklist can protect you better than panic-driven reactions after money is already gone.

Quick Answer

Never share OTP, PIN, CVV, or app access with anyone. Never trust payment requests just because the caller sounds official. Slow down, verify independently, and assume urgency is a risk signal until proven otherwise.

10 Practical Rules

  1. Never share OTP, PIN, CVV, or card details.
  2. Do not click payment or KYC links sent on calls or random messages.
  3. Do not install unknown apps for "support" or "refund" help.
  4. Verify bank, courier, or wallet issues through official channels only.
  5. Read UPI requests carefully before approving.
  6. Ignore pressure tactics like "account will close in 10 minutes".
  7. Keep transaction alerts on.
  8. Use strong screen lock and app lock.
  9. Review statements for small suspicious debits too.
  10. Tell family members these rules, especially older users.

Common Scam Patterns

PatternWhat to do
Fake KYC updateIgnore the link and use only official app or website.
Refund or cashback requestNever approve collect request without understanding it.
Remote access support callDo not install any app under caller pressure.
Prize, reward, or job advance feeStop immediately and verify independently.

After a Suspicious Incident

  1. Freeze or block the payment instrument if needed.
  2. Change password and app PIN.
  3. Check recent transactions.
  4. Contact the bank or platform through official support.
  5. Document what happened while details are fresh.
