Bank Fraud Complaint Steps: What to Do in the First 30 Minutes

Last updated: April 8, 2026

In a fraud situation, speed matters more than perfect thinking. Protect the account first, save evidence second, and use only official support channels. A structured 30-minute response plan reduces confusion and can reduce damage.

Quick Answer

Block the payment instrument or secure account access immediately, then report through the bank's official app, helpline, or branch channel. Save screenshots, transaction details, and complaint reference numbers at once.

First 30-Minute Action Plan

  1. Confirm the suspicious transaction is not yours.
  2. Lock card, UPI, or account access using official tools.
  3. Change password or app PIN if compromise is possible.
  4. Report through the official bank channel.
  5. Write down complaint number, date, and time.
  6. Save screenshots of alerts and transaction details.

Common Mistakes

MistakeWhy it hurts
Calling random search-result numbersFake support scams target panic.
Deleting alertsYou lose evidence.
Waiting too longMore attempts may happen.
Sharing OTP to reverse the fraudThe loss can deepen immediately.
