Health Insurance Basics: Deductible, Co-pay, and Coverage Limits

Last updated: March 1, 2026

Health insurance is not only about premium cost. Claim rules decide whether a policy helps during an emergency. Learn the key terms before buying or renewing.

Quick Answer

Choose policy by claim usability: room-rent rule, co-pay, waiting period, and network hospitals, not just low premium.

Core Terms You Must Check

  • Deductible: Amount you pay first before insurer contributes.
  • Co-pay: Fixed percentage of bill you pay on every claim.
  • Waiting Period: Time before specific illnesses are covered.
  • Sub-limits: Caps on room rent or treatment categories.

Practical Selection Rules

  1. Prefer plans with broad hospital network in your city.
  2. Avoid high co-pay unless premium savings are substantial.
  3. Read exclusions for pre-existing disease and maternity.
  4. Keep emergency contact and policy ID easily accessible.

Common Mistakes

  • Buying only tax-saving policy without coverage analysis.
  • Ignoring claim settlement process and cashless support.
  • Missing renewal date and losing continuity benefits.

Final Takeaway

Cheap premium can be expensive at claim time. Verify coverage conditions first.
